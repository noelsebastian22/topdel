# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Australian homeowners planning a considered, made-to-measure storage or joinery project — walk-in and built-in wardrobes, home office / study fitouts, laundry cabinetry, and custom street-address face plates. Some are mid-renovation or building new; others are upgrading one room they've lived with too long.

Their context when they arrive: they've likely seen flat-pack and big-box options and want something better, but they're wary of both cowboy tradies and inflated "designer" quotes. They're comparing makers and need proof of quality before they'll pick up the phone. The decision is high-consideration and personal — this joinery lives in their home for years — so trust has to be earned before the enquiry.

## Product Purpose

A lead-generation marketing site for TopDel House Decor, a custom joinery / house-decor business. It exists to convert a cautious homeowner into an enquiry by **building trust first**: real completed-project photography and a clear, honest account of how the work is measured, made and installed do the persuading, and the enquiry (a phone call to 043 000 6015 or the contact form) is the payoff once the visitor is convinced.

Success looks like qualified enquiries from homeowners who value bespoke craft and have already decided TopDel is the maker they want — not raw click volume. The gallery is the engine; the call/form is the finish line.

## Brand Personality

Premium, refined, bespoke — but grounded in a real, hands-on maker, never luxury-by-costume. Voice is a straight-talking Australian craftsperson: precise (millimetre-accurate, "wall to wall, no filler panels"), quietly confident, plain-spoken. It should feel like the work of someone who measures twice and cuts once, and whose taste you'd trust in your own home.

The elevation is deliberate: the site should read a notch more considered and bespoke than a typical trade site, with that "premium" quality carried by precision, restraint, and the quality of the imagery — not by gold, ornament, or marketing gloss.

## Anti-references

- **Generic IKEA / flat-pack.** This is custom, made-to-measure joinery cut to the room's real dimensions — nothing about it should read as catalogue modular units or mass-market flat-pack.
- **Cheap tradie clip-art.** No cluttered local-tradie template: no spinning badges, stock clip-art, starbursts, or "10 YEARS EXPERIENCE!" noise.
- **Corporate SaaS / startup.** No gradient hero, feature-card grid, stock-illustration people, or sterile product-landing tropes. This is a maker, not a software product.
- **Overwrought luxury.** No gold-serif, over-styled luxury affectation. Refinement here is honest and hands-on; inauthentic opulence would undercut the maker's credibility.

## Design Principles

- **Show the work, don't claim it.** Real project photography is the primary trust instrument; copy stays spare and lets the joinery prove the point. Zero-imagery restraint is failure, not elegance.
- **Precision is the luxury.** The premium/bespoke feel comes from exactness and restraint — measured detail, millimetre honesty, clean alignment — not from ornament. Elevate the existing "shop drawing / blueprint" language toward refinement; don't swap it for luxury cliché.
- **Made-to-measure, never catalogue.** Every section reinforces custom, one-off, cut-to-your-room — the opposite of flat-pack.
- **Earn the enquiry, then make it effortless.** Sequence the page so trust builds toward a low-friction next step; calling or filling the form should feel like the obvious move at each point of conviction, never a hard sell.
- **Local and real.** Plain Australian English, a business you can actually ring — credibility comes from being specific and grounded, not polished-generic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text ≥4.5:1 and large text ≥3:1 against their backgrounds (watch mono spec-labels and any text over imagery/scrims); full keyboard operability with the existing visible focus ring; `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for the hero video, scroll parallax, and reveal animations (already scaffolded — keep it). Gallery alt text stays descriptive and specific, as it is now, since it doubles as brand voice. Copy in Australian English (`en-AU`).
